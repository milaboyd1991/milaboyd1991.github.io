About Mila Boyd.

I didn't start designing to make beautiful things. I started designing to solve a feeling. The feeling of standing in a space that doesn't feel like you.

I am not a single thing. I am a system.

I'm Mila Boyd. Most people arrive at design with one skill. I arrived with a life's worth of observation. Growing up between cultures taught me to read rooms before anyone spoke, to sense what a space communicated, what a person needed, what an environment was quietly saying about the people who lived inside it.

That sensitivity became my advantage. Before I knew the words visual hierarchy, Gestalt psychology, or UX/UI, I already understood them emotionally. I understood when something felt expensive versus disorganized, when a business felt trustworthy or uncertain, when a space felt like refuge or like noise.

Today, I channel everything I have ever learned (design, psychology, technology, flooring, branding, emotional intelligence, systems thinking) into one focused purpose: helping people build environments and digital products that feel exactly like who they intend to be.

The Journey

I started on the floor.

Literally. Measuring rooms, pulling samples, standing next to homeowners as they made the most expensive decision of their year. I moved up through senior design roles at Floor & Decor, CS Floors and Home Depot, eventually carrying $945K+ in annual sales and a 25% store contribution rate. First and only designer in store history to reach it (store average 16 to 17 percent). Now I am studying UX/UI at Pierce College and rebuilding that same rigor inside a digital practice.
