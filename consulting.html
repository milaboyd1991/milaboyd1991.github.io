What I can do.

Six ways I move a project forward
01 · Research

Market & Resource Research

I build the fact base. Operators, partners, candidates, agencies, and programs gathered from verified public sources, with a source link and a confidence note on every entry.

No guessed contacts, no "I heard this." Each claim ties back to an official page or a document so decisions hold up under scrutiny.

What's included
  • Operator, partner, and candidate databases with source links
  • Government and program research with eligibility notes
  • Due-diligence question sets and verification checklists
  • Comparison tables and shortlists with risk flags
  • A source directory you can hand to a partner or attorney
Best fit

Founders entering a new market, sector, or country who need the landscape mapped before they spend money or make promises.

02 · Systems

CRM & Pipeline Systems

I set up the operating system for the opportunity in Airtable, Notion, or Monday so no contact, document, or follow-up is ever lost.

Every contact carries a source, a status, a risk note, an owner, and a next action. The project stops living in text messages.

What's included
  • Contact and pipeline tracker with custom fields and tags
  • Linked tables for partners, candidates, programs, and legal
  • Status, risk, owner, and next-action on every record
  • A secure, permissioned data room for documents
  • A weekly report: wins, risks, meetings, decisions needed
Best fit

Teams juggling many leads and documents who need one searchable, evidence-based source of truth.

03 · Capital

Government & Export Resource Navigation

I map the real, eligible support, not the myth of free money. SBA and export finance, STEP reimbursements, EXIM, SBDC counseling, U.S. Commercial Service matchmaking, and woman-owned certification pathways.

I draft the right questions, identify the correct office, and schedule the calls so you ask for what actually exists.

What's included
  • A capital and export-support map for your specific activity
  • Program eligibility notes and the questions to ask each office
  • Contact routing for SBA, SBDC, EXIM, and Commercial Service
  • WOSB / EDWOSB and supplier-diversity positioning research
  • A capital stack and lean first-budget framework
Best fit

Exporters and small businesses who want to find real public support and stop chasing grants that will not arrive.

04 · People

Recruiting & Vendor Coordination

I turn "we need help" into a written role profile, a shortlist of the right specialist recruiters and firms, and an intake process that respects verification.

I coordinate the search and keep the credential-verification rule in front of everyone before anyone is presented to a client.

What's included
  • Written role profiles and staffing-order planning
  • Specialist recruiter and firm shortlists by role
  • Intake scripts, fee questions, and replacement-guarantee notes
  • Candidate trackers with references and verification status
  • A clear line between coordination and licensed/regulated work
Best fit

Founders who need to fill expert gaps with the right contractors first, not expensive permanent payroll.

05 · Documents

Documents, Decks & Outreach

I turn verified information into professional artifacts: multi-page manuals, one-page briefs, pitch decks, outreach scripts, and email templates that read clear, modest, and credible.

As managing editor I own tone, brand, and source control, and I can direct a writer or designer when the scope needs one.

What's included
  • Execution manuals, playbooks, and SOPs
  • One-page opportunity and partner briefs
  • Investor and partner pitch decks
  • Outreach scripts and email templates by audience
  • Meeting agendas, minutes, and decision memos
Best fit

Anyone who needs to look ready and organized for an investor, a government office, or a serious partner.

06 · Operations

Project Operations

I run the cadence. A 30-day sprint to a professional, evidence-backed package, a 90-day roadmap to a go / no-go decision, and a meeting rhythm where every call ends with one next action and one owner.

I also build the guardrails: written scope, confidentiality steps, and honest "if eligible" language that protects everyone.

What's included
  • 30-day sprint plan and 90-day implementation roadmap
  • Meeting checklists: agenda, minutes, action items, follow-up
  • Due-diligence and go / no-go decision frameworks
  • Risk and compliance guardrails with safe language
  • A clean division of labor between you and your partners
Best fit

Founders who have the relationship but need the discipline to turn it into a fundable, decision-ready plan.

Case study: a 250-page execution program.

Selected work · Confidential client

A consulting client held a letter of intent for a private-aviation partnership in Kuwait but no plan to execute it. I built the entire business-development program that turned that opening into an actionable file, eight documents spanning more than 250 pages, delivered in English and Arabic.

A 100-page master buildout plan, a 50-page government-capital and staffing manual, a 45-page role and resource directory, a 25-page funding and staffing path, a consolidated bilingual research dossier, a professional guide, a hiring and licensing addendum, and a ranked operator shortlist. The program separated what the government may realistically help with from what must be paid privately, mapped the loan, reimbursement, and counseling options, listed the strongest staffing and legal resources, and laid out controlled 30 and 90-day sprints, all on a verified public-source directory.

What this is, and isn't.

Honest about it

This is business-development and planning support: research, documents, systems, coordination, and resource navigation. It is not legal advice, regulatory approval, securities advice, or a guarantee of any loan, grant, permit, or contract.

Formal commitments belong with qualified attorneys, licensed professionals, and the appropriate government offices. I make those people easier to find, easier to brief, and easier to act on.

Working in a regulated field? I build the package and the verification trail so your specialists can do their part faster, and so nobody overpromises along the way.
