Roots & Vision

I am not a single thing. I am a system.

Most people arrive at design with one skill. I arrived with a life's worth of observation. Growing up between cultures taught me to read rooms before anyone spoke. That sensitivity became the practice.

01 · Roots

Where the work comes from.

For more than ten years I designed physical spaces. Flooring, tile, kitchens, bathrooms, wet areas, solid surfaces. Sitting at long showroom tables with homeowners as they made the most expensive decision of their year. Choosing a slab. Holding two material samples in different light. Saying, this one. Trusting it.

That work taught me something most digital designers never learn. How decisions feel after they are built and lived in. How a small wrong choice compounds. How a small right choice quietly carries a home for a decade.

I design with the slow eye of someone who started on the floor. That patience is now my UX.
02 · The Pivot

August 7, 2025.

On August 7, 2025, I was fired for standing by my values. I will not hide that. It was the most clarifying day of my life.

Within two months I had built PNW Floor Design LLC, partnered with contractors and suppliers, sold heated floors and tile and windows, been hired as a Senior Designer at Floor & Decor at a higher salary, been invited to Phi Theta Kappa, and started studying UX/UI in earnest.

I tell this not as a hardship story. I tell it because the practice you are reading about today was built in the months after that day. Calm under pressure is not a slogan in this studio. It is the architecture.

03 · Vision

Where this is going.

I am building a studio that does the work most flooring companies will not. Visual systems. Customer experience. Calm communication. The boring parts of business done with the same care as the beautiful parts.

The studio takes a small number of clients each quarter. Founders, small studios, and homeowners who want clarity, warmth, and a system that lasts. If that sounds like the work you are looking for, the next step is a short call.
