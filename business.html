Brand Design · Case Study

Lacey Legacy, designing a luxury studio identity from scratch.

A self-directed brand and web identity project. I designed a fictional creative studio end-to-end, name, palette, type system, logo treatment, service architecture, web layout, to test how far I could push a quiet-luxury aesthetic inside a real Bootstrap build.

Role Brand Designer + Web Designer
Type Self-directed concept project
Tools Figma · HTML · CSS · Bootstrap
Year 2026

The Brief

Design a small, quiet-luxury creative studio identity that could believably take on UX/UI, brand, and photography clients. The site had to feel calm and considered on first read, build trust before asking for action, and survive being built in plain Bootstrap without losing its high-end feel.

My Approach

I started with the feeling, not the components. Three words shaped every decision: warm action, luxury trust, visual novel. From those, I built the palette, picked a serif-italic display face, set a generous baseline, and let space and color do most of the work. Components came last.

Design Decisions

Color, type, and the feeling underneath.

Palette

A soft, dusk-leaning system. Blue carries trust, peach softens the edges, pink keeps it human, sage grounds it, and the ink-burgundy holds everything in place. I avoided pure black, too sharp for the warmth I wanted.

Type System

Playfair Display italic carries the personality. Source Serif Pro carries the reading. Lato handles the small mechanical work, eyebrows, buttons, nav. Three voices, each with a clear job.

Outcome & Reflection

What I learned building it.

Lacey Legacy was never going to launch as a real business, I'm building toward a different brand for that work. But the exercise gave me something more useful than a client deliverable: a working test of how far quiet-luxury thinking can go inside plain Bootstrap, with no JavaScript animation frameworks and no premium fonts.

The biggest lesson was about restraint. Most luxury cues are subtractive, fewer colors, fewer weights, more space, more silence. The harder skill wasn't designing the screens. It was deleting the parts that almost made it in.

The system I built here became the template for thinking about my future business brand: choose the feeling first, write three words down, and let the components earn their place against that test.

"Warm action, luxury trust, visual novel, the three threads I wove through every single decision in this brand."

, Mila Boyd, Designer
