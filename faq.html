FAQ

The questions I get most.

Honest answers about scope, timing, payment, and how the studio actually runs. If something is missing, send it through the inquiry form.

01

How do projects usually start?

You send a few sentences through the inquiry form. I respond within two business days. If the fit feels right, we book a thirty minute call. After the call, you get a written brief and a fixed scope. If we both say yes, the engagement starts on a date we agree on together.

02

How long does a project take?

Most engagements run between six and twelve weeks. Larger remodels and full brand systems stretch longer. Tiny scopes finish faster. I confirm the timeline in writing during Discover so there are no surprises.

03

What does the studio cost?

Strategy sessions start at $750. Most focused engagements land between $4K and $12K. Long form work for complex projects starts at $18K. Full pricing detail on the Pricing page.

04

Do you work remote or in person?

Most engagements run remote with weekly video sessions. For interior and remodel work in the Pacific Northwest, I do site visits when the project benefits from them. Travel beyond the PNW is quoted separately.

05

Will I work with you directly?

Yes. There is no account manager and no junior pass off. The person reading your brief is the person making the design decisions. The studio is intentionally small so every project gets full attention.

06

How does payment work?

Fifty percent up front secures the start date. The remainder is paid on handoff, or split across milestones for longer engagements. Invoices are sent through standard tools. Card, bank, or international wire all accepted.

07

What if the project changes mid stream?

Scope changes are normal. They get written down and quoted. The original scope holds at the original price. New scope is agreed in writing before any new work starts. No surprises on either side.

08

Do you use AI?

Yes, as a collaborator, never as the brand. AI helps with research synthesis, first drafts, and pattern checks. Every design decision and every word of brand voice is made by a human. The designer holds the system. The system holds the work.

09

Do you take pro bono or non profit work?

One pro bono project per year for a mission aligned non profit. Applications open in January. The studio commits the same scope discipline to pro bono engagements as to paid ones.

10

What if you say no?

If the studio is not the right fit, I say so quickly and recommend a designer or studio who is. The answer comes within two business days of the inquiry. No drawn out conversations that go nowhere.
